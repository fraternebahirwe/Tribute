Tribute to E.P Lumumba

Emery Patrice Lumumba

Patrice Lumumba (1925–1961) was a Congolese nationalist leader and a pivotal figure in the independence of the Democratic Republic of the Congo. As the first democratically elected Prime Minister in 1960, his powerful independence day speech—which boldly denounced the humiliations and crimes of colonialism—earned him the hostility of Western powers during the Cold War. He was assassinated on January 17, 1961, following a coup, and has since become a revered pan-African martyr and a global symbol of resistance against imperialism.

Achievements

  • Founding of the MNC
  • Struggle for immediate independence
  • Independence Day speech (Independence Act speech)
  • Defense of national unity
